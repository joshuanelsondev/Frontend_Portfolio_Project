Game Rules

  • In this game of 21 the player will play against the computer who will also serve as the dealer.
  • We will be using a standard deck of 52 cards, so no jokers!
  • The Objective is for you, the player, to have your cards amount to 21 or as close as possible without going over.
  • To count the cards each card is assigned a value:
    • Number cards are worth their face value. A 2 is worth 2 points, a 3 is worth 3 points and so on, all the way up to 10.
    • The king, queen, and jack are worth 10 points.
    • The Ace is worth 1 point or 11 points, with the decision being left to the player.
  • The player will be dealt 2 cards that will remain face up while the dealer will have their first card showing and the second one face down.
  • The player will have the option to HIT (draw another card) as long as they are not over 21 points.
  • In this version of 21 the player must HIT if their points are under 17, the same rule goes for the dealer.
  • Once the player decides to pass their turn to the dealer the dealer's face down card will be revealed and they will have the opportunity to draw or not.
  • If the player or dealer have 21 when the cards are dealt then they automatically win.
  • The game results in a tie when both the player and the dealer have 21
  • In the event of the player and dealer having the same score but both being under 21 points, the player must draw another card.
